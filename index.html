I Love The Whole World

But it needs to be better. I'm driven by the concept of Effective Altruism; the idea that, with careful examination, some philosophy, and a lot of science, we can optimize the amount of good we do for the world to improve it by orders of magnitude. For example, GiveWell identifies charities that are a thousand times more cost-effective at saving lives, yet are less funded than others.

In December 2014, I graduated The Iron Yard Academy, a 12-week immersive course focused on web development with Ruby on Rails. I now hope to utilize my skills and experience to either work directly on projects that steer the course of history towards Effective Altruism, or donate a significant amount of my income to EA organizations.

Before my decision to take the plunge into programming and web development is a winding path of diverse experience. In the preceding three years, I was a Project Manager for the Localization team at Disney Social Games, promoted there from QA Analyst. I've worked on dozens of social, mobile, and casual games at my time there, and at Playdom, Merscom, and Funcom (yes I ended up at three successive, unrelated game companies with one-word two-syllable names ending in "-om"). And kicking it all off was a BA in Art & Design from the College of Design at NCSU.


Projects for The Iron Yard Academy


Clipboard for SEEDS

on GitHub

Clipboard is a simple, intuitive volunteer check-in app created specifically for SEEDS, an NC environmental non-profit. The web app saves time and money by both making the check-in process as straightforward as possible, and aiding in capturing volunteer time data, vital to qualify for grants needed to sustain and grow their programs.

Clipboard integrates with a Salesforce database via API, accommodates the existing idiosynchrocies in data structure, automatically handles complexities previously needing a human in the loop, and serves only what is necessary for a smooth, swift user experience.

Clipboard was developed over the course of three weeks, by Rebecca Conley, Pete Byrne, and I at The Iron Yard Academy. Developing Clipboard was a great chance to work with a client, get our hands dirty with real-world data, and optimize a solution for specific and evolving requirements.

Game Design


Fog of War

Rules PDF

A rare example of a recreational game design project that attained a state I'm not embarassed to share. My design goals were to require only a traditional deck of playing cards, elegance of the ruleset, and the core hidden movement mechanic that inspired it. Players take turns taking 7 cards in their hand, some of which may be army cards, and placing them back down without their opponent knowing the exact positioning and layout.
