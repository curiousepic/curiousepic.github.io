I Love The Whole World

But thinks it needs to be better. I'm driven by the concept of Effective Altruism; the idea that, with careful examination, some philosophy, and a lot of science, we can optimize the amount of good we do for the world to improve it by orders of magnitude. For example, GiveWell identifies charities a thousand times more cost-effective at saving lives, yet are less funded than others.

I hope to either work directly on projects that steer the course of history to this path, or donate a significant amount of my income to organizations that do.

Previously a Project Manager for the Localization team at Disney Social Games, I realized programming is an incredibly powerful means to this end, and finally took the plunge by joining The Iron Yard Academy to learn web development.


Projects for The Iron Yard Academy


Clipboard for SEEDS

on GitHub

Clipboard is a simple, intuitive volunteer check-in app created specifically for SEEDS, an NC environmental non-profit. The web app saves time and money by both making the check-in process as straightforward as possible, and aiding in capturing volunteer time data, vital to qualify for grants needed to sustain and grow their programs.

Clipboard integrates with a Salesforce database via API, accommodates the existing idiosynchrocies in data structure, automatically handles complexities previously needing a human in the loop, and serves only what is necessary for a smooth, swift user experience.

Clipboard was developed over the course of three weeks, by Rebecca Conley, Pete Byrne, and I at The Iron Yard Academy. Developing Clipboard was a great chance to work with a client, get our hands dirty with real-world data, and optimize a solution for specific and evolving requirements.

HALP

on GitHub

Our week 6 project was to expand a skeletal project used for making homework help requests. It was fully test-driven and involved setting up a mailer for registration verification and notifications, file uploads, as well as our first deployment on Heroku.
